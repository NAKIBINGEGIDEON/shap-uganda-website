Who We Are

KAN Sustainable Health Action for Development (SHAP-Uganda) Limited is an indigenous, not-for-profit, non-governmental organization founded in 2021 by a dedicated team of public health professionals. Our mission is to influence development and deliver sustainable, integrated, and high-quality comprehensive health care services to marginalized and vulnerable individuals through capacity building, community empowerment, and participatory health interventions.

Where We Work

We operate in the districts of Kamuli, Luuka, Buyende, Kayunga, and Namutumba. Our programs address:

  • Integrated Reproductive Maternal, Newborn, Child, and Adolescent Health Services
  • Prevention and Control of Communicable and Non-Communicable Diseases
  • Health Promotion and Community Health Initiatives
  • Environmental and School Health Programs
  • Health System Strengthening
  • Gender and Health Interventions

Our target beneficiaries include:

  • Adolescent Girls, Boys, and Young Women (AGYW)
  • Orphans and Vulnerable Children (OVC)
  • Women and Youth
  • Key and Priority Populations
  • People Living with HIV (PLHIV)
  • People Living with Disabilities (PLWD)

Vision, Mission, and Core Values

Vision: A transformed community for better health outcomes.

Mission: To improve accessibility to sustainable quality health care by empowering vulnerable communities to embrace health services and strengthening existing structures at various levels.

Core Values:

  • Transparency: Commitment to openness in sharing intentions, funding availability, and resource accountability.
  • Inclusiveness & Equity: Non-discrimination across gender, age, ethnicity, religion, disability, or socio-economic status.
  • Professionalism & Probity: Upholding the highest ethical standards and fostering respect, communication, and dedication.
  • Innovativeness & Sustainability: Encouraging originality and active community participation for lasting impact.
  • Accountability: Responsibility for organizational performance, funds, and stakeholder trust.

Principles

  • Prevention: Focused interventions for disease prevention and early detection.
  • Efficiency: Optimal resource use to maximize health outcomes.
  • Primacy of Localism: Engaging community gatekeepers for active participation in health initiatives.

Objectives

Strategic Objectives:

  • Strengthening community health systems and promoting health through capacity building, research, and structural development.
  • Empowering communities economically by addressing gender inclusion, child protection, and climate change adaptation.

Specific Objectives:

  • Promote awareness, availability, and accessibility of health care services.
  • Support the Government of Uganda in implementing the Minimum Primary Health Care Package.
  • Advocate for a socially, economically, legally, and politically empowered community.
  • Collaborate with stakeholders to ensure quality health care delivery.
  • Contribute to child protection services within communities.

Our Stakeholders

SHAP-Uganda collaborates with a wide range of partners to achieve its mission, including:

  • Ministry of Health and Ministry of Education
  • Development partners
  • District Local Governments
  • Religious and Cultural Leaders
  • Schools and Health Facilities
  • Community-Based Organizations (CBOs) and Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs)
